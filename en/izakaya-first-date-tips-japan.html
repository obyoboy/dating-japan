Is an Izakaya a Good First Date Spot in Japan?

Izakayas are relaxed, affordable, and everywhere — but are they the right choice for a first date with a Japanese woman? Here's what you need to know.

Last updated: 2026-04-10  |  6 min read

1. What Is an Izakaya?

An izakaya (居酒屋) is a Japanese gastropub where people go to drink and share small plates of food. Think of it as a cross between a bar and a casual restaurant. They range from chain establishments like Torikizoku to intimate mom-and-pop spots tucked into side alleys. Most have low lighting, a lively atmosphere, and a menu built around sharing — which actually makes them socially convenient for a date.

2. Why an Izakaya Can Work for a First Date

The relaxed, informal setting takes away some of the pressure that comes with a formal dinner. You don't have to worry about dress codes, and there's always something happening around you if the conversation hits a lull. Ordering food together is a natural icebreaker — you get to learn each other's tastes without a script.

Izakayas are also relatively affordable. Splitting the bill (warikan) or covering the tab won't feel financially heavy, which keeps the evening low-stakes and comfortable for both of you.

Tip Choose a semi-private booth or a counter seat rather than a large shared table. Quieter seating makes conversation much easier on a first date.

3. Potential Downsides to Consider

Izakayas are often loud, especially on Friday and Saturday nights. If she's on the shy side or if you're not yet comfortable with Japanese, a noisy environment can make communication harder. Some women also associate izakayas primarily with work drinking parties (nomikai), which gives the venue a less romantic connotation for them.

Warning Avoid choosing a rowdy chain izakaya on a weekend night for a first date. The noise level can make it feel like a group party rather than a one-on-one conversation.

4. How to Pick the Right Izakaya

Not all izakayas are the same. A well-chosen spot can feel genuinely intimate and special. Look for these qualities when selecting a venue:

FeatureWhat to Look For
SeatingBooths or private rooms (個室, ko-shitsu)
Noise levelModerate — you can talk without raising your voice
AtmosphereWarm lighting, wood decor, clean interior
MenuVariety of food, non-alcoholic drink options
LocationEasy to reach by train, safe neighborhood

Searching for 個室居酒屋 (private room izakaya) near your area is a great way to find spots that feel more date-appropriate.

5. Drinking Etiquette on a First Date

Japan has a strong drinking culture, but don't assume she drinks. Always ask what she'd like rather than automatically ordering beer for the table. Many izakayas have excellent non-alcoholic options like sparkling water, yuzu soda, or non-alcoholic cocktails. Never pressure her to drink more than she wants — this is a dealbreaker for many Japanese women.

If you do drink, pace yourself. Getting visibly drunk on a first date signals poor self-control and leaves a bad impression that is hard to undo.

6. Food Ordering Tips

Ordering food together is one of the best parts of an izakaya date. Ask her what she likes before ordering, and let her take the lead if she's familiar with the menu. Common crowd-pleasers include edamame, karaage, yakitori, and potato salad. If she has dietary restrictions — some women avoid certain meats or seafood — check before ordering a large spread.

Tip Suggesting she pick a few dishes shows consideration and makes her feel comfortable. It also tells you a lot about her personality and tastes.

7. The Bill: Who Pays?

This is a common question for foreign men dating in Japan. There's no single rule, but on a first date, offering to pay the full bill is generally well-received and seen as a gentlemanly gesture. Some women will insist on splitting; accept gracefully if she does. What matters most is how you handle the moment — do it naturally and without making a production of it.

8. Final Verdict

An izakaya can be an excellent first date spot if you choose wisely. Pick a quieter venue with private seating, be attentive to her comfort, and let the informal atmosphere work in your favor. It's far better than a rigid formal dinner for breaking the ice — just avoid the loudest chain spots on busy nights.
